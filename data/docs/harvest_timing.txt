GROUNDNUT HARVEST TIMING - NIGERIA
Source: ICRISAT/IAR Farmer's Guide to Groundnut Production in Nigeria (2015)

Harvest groundnut when 70-80% of pods have matured.
Key harvest indicator: darkening of the internal surface of the pericarp - shells turning dark brown inside.
Seed coat should be thin and tight on the kernel.
Early harvesting lowers yield, oil quantity and seed quality.
Delayed harvesting causes pod loss due to weakening of pegs and increases aflatoxin risk.
In Sudan savanna zone: harvest as soon as rainfall ceases to avoid soil hardening.
Harvesting is advisable when seeds are plump and show true color of the variety.
